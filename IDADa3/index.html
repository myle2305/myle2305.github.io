PROJECT OVERVIEW

My final UI combines the creative prompts: Spatial Control and advanced techniques: Math.random(). The project's goal is to create a galaxy with stars that change with every load and click, allowing users to interact with a spirit of experimentation and exploration.

1. DESIGN VALUES

Learnability

Sound Galaxy's first design value is learnability, ensuring users can understand and interact with the system naturally without complicated instructions.

User Guidance: The first screen only displays 4 core information: "12 Zodiac Constellations", "Click on the Stars", "Make Sounds", "Scroll to Explore", helping users understand the content inside and how the website works. The detailed instructions panel is hidden by default, only showing when users need help by clicking the button or pressing the H key. In addition, the arrows on the keyboard can be used to navigate the space, allowing desktop users to easily access interactions without a touchpad.

Intuitive Affordances: Each star has a pointer and can hover, signaling that it is interactive. The hover effect zooms in 2.2 times with a bright light, providing strong feedback before clicking. The constellation stars are larger (12-20px) than the background stars (3-5px) to help users easily recognize and interact with the desired element, and also as a hint for users to find all 12 constellations.

Playfulness

The second design value is playfulness. I want users to feel free to experiment and explore without the pressure of completing a goal. Each interaction should be a small surprise, pique curiosity and stimulate the user to experience.

Non-linear Exploration: The 10000x6000px galaxy creates a feeling of endless expansion. 12 constellations are randomly distributed and spread throughout the background allowing users to freely explore. Each play can have a completely different journey.

Generative Music System: The background stars are randomly assigned notes from the C3-G4 scale. Users can click on any star to freely create notes. There is no wrong note, each play session creates a unique sound because the notes are randomly arranged.

const ambientNotes = ["C3", "D3", "E3", "F3", "G3", "A3",
                  "B3", "C4", "D4", "E4", "F4", "G4"];
const randomNote = ambientNotes[
  Math.floor(Math.random() * ambientNotes.length)
];

2. INTERACTION DESIGN RATIONALE

Feedback Design

The project's feedback following the principle of "Show, Don't Tell", every interaction must have clear responses rather than relying on text instructions.

Visual Feedback: Background stars flash yellow (scale 3x) for 300ms when clicked. Constellation stars turn gold permanently and pulse infinitely when activated, providing spatial memory and showing progress visually.

Audio Feedback: Background stars play a short sound, constellation stars play a medium sound, and completing a constellation plays a small symphonies to indicate that the user has found a constellation.

Information Design

The help toggle, volume control, and scroll indicator are the only three UI elements that are always visible. By default, the constellation information and help panel are hidden and only show up once request. To match the galaxy theme, all stars used yellow on a dark background.

Visual Hierarchy: Z-index layers ensure that constellation stars (z-index: 10) are always clickable, lines (z-index: 5) are visible but don't block the stars, and background stars (z-index: 1) fill the space without being cluttered.

3. SIGNIFICANT CHANGES FROM PROTOTYPE & PRODUCTION TIMELINE

Math.random() Expansion

Evolved from basic positioning to randomization across 6 aspects:

  • Star size: 3 + Math.random() * 2
  • Position: Math.random() * 10000
  • Animation delay: Math.random() * 3 seconds
  • Animation duration: 2 + Math.random() * 4 seconds
  • Notes: Math.floor(Math.random() * notes.length)
  • Position offset: (Math.random() - 0.5) * 20 pixels

Audio System

I refined the feedback sounds a bit, adding some effects to them to make them sound more airy, not as muddy as the prototype version.

Constellations

I also implemented the creation of connected constellations and symphonies feedback after the user clicks all the stars in that constellation.

Comparision with prototype

Compared to the previous prototype, my final UI has made significant progress. It was mainly a matter of building on the old one and fixing the shortcomings that I had received feedback from peer testing. In the UI prototype, the scrolling was broken and the galaxy looked too crowded and cluttered, the star area was too small to interact with. In addition, simplifying the website by cutting out the guide for users to freely explore seemed unfeasible, leaving users confused about how the website interacted. I thought it was a good solution because it created a spacious and unbroken galaxy of UI elements, but feedback from actual users proved my thinking wrong. So, I tried to fix the confusing interface by adding a guide and increasing the size of both the stars and their selections, while reducing the number of stars to avoid feeling suffocated. And this change is the right one, the interface looks more natural and the interaction is also easier. In addition, I had the idea that users would click on the stars until they found a star belonging to a certain constellation, then they would automatically connect together and play a series of sounds. But perhaps this is impossible, because with a large background size and a large number of stars spread across the background, it would be difficult and frustrating if users kept clicking without knowing when they hit a certain constellation. Therefore, I designed the zodiac stars to be significantly larger than the stars in the background, ensuring that users can easily recognize them. When users click enough stars belonging to that constellation, they will play a small symphony to respond to the user. The zodiac constellations are also simplified by me, in fact, there are zodiac constellations that are a collection of many stars and the shapes are quite complex, which is not suitable for simple interactive UI design, moreover, it will create a chaotic harmony and cause fatigue for users if they have to click too many times. Therefore, I think it would be better to create constellations that represent the zodiac signs, which can both satisfy the visual and auditory needs and avoid causing discomfort to the user experience.

Spent more time than expected:

  • The line-drawing algorithm proved that simple math doesn’t guarantee easy implementation. To be honest, it wasn’t easy to implement, so I relied heavily on chatGPT. The random star size meant the center wasn’t in the top left corner, requiring centerX and centerY values to be calculated and stored. A CSS transform origin issue caused all the corners to be 90 degrees off, which took a fair amount of debugging. After fixing the math, it had to be checked in the browser to make sure it matched up visually. This taught me that mathematical correctness doesn’t equal visual correctness, and cross-browser testing should be done early on.
  • Refining the sound system required finding the right sound for the concept through experimentation. I eventually found the sound that worked best, after experimenting with the attack times, release times of the notes. It is important to test the sound in context as sounds that work on their own often do not work well when layered with other sounds.
  • The scrolling system demonstrated the power of leveraging the browser's built-in features. Instead of implementing custom camera motion with position tracking, momentum physics, and easy calculations, I simply set the container size to 10000x6000px, added overflow: scroll, and used window.scrollBy() with behavior: 'smooth' for keyboard controls. The browser automatically handled smooth scrolling physics, mouse wheel support, touch gestures, momentum on Mac, scroll position memory, and performance optimizations.

Challenge:

I created a branch for the prototype and continued editing on the main branch for the final UI, but there was a problem that I could not open the prototype on the browser because it had the same name as the UI that I was continuing to work on. And the prototype branch to netlify failed because in the prototype I did not have the index.html file as require, resulting in a "files not found" screen. And I chose the solution of copying the code of the prototype branch to a separate new folder to commit to github. This carelessness led to an inefficient and time-consuming work process. This is a shortcoming that I need to recognize and learn from in future projects.

4. DESIGN ASPECT I'M MOST PROUD OF

The Math.random() Symphonies System

I'm most proud of how Math.random() used in a simple but powerful way. Instead of just using randomness as decoration, it can be functional and be the main component for the user to interact with.

1. Size: 3 + Math.random() * 2
2. Position X: Math.random() * 10000
3. Position Y: Math.random() * 6000
4. Animation Delay: Math.random() * 3
5. Animation Duration: 2 + Math.random() * 4
6. Note: notes[Math.floor(Math.random() * 12)]

Each random element can compounds with others. A star isn't just randomly positioned, it's also randomly sized, randomly timed, and randomly voiced:

  • 5 size variations × 800 positions × 3 timing ranges × 12 notes = millions of unique combinations
  • Yet it all feels coherent because ranges are carefully bounded
  • The galaxy feels natural despite being fully algorithmic

The constellation offset is very important because it can unintentionally cause unnatural and unbalanced designs:

const offsetX = (Math.random() - 0.5) * 20;
const offsetY = (Math.random() - 0.5) * 20;

The natural placement of the stars goes a long way in making the galaxy look realistic. Additionally, each time the site loads, the stars and constellations are randomly placed, which adds to the user experience every time they come back to interact with the site.

5. MOST CHALLENGING ASPECT

Balancing Playfulness with Clarity

The biggest challenge in the design process was how to balance the need to promote playfulness while still ensuring learning, as playfulness requires surprise and discovery, and learning requires a clear, easy-to-grasp interface. This required careful compromises across the entire application. For example, the background stars were made interactive, making sounds and lights when clicked to encourage exploration. Their distinct small size and non-fixed feedback were used to differentiate them from the main constellation mechanic. Additionally, a good balance was found for the tutorial: instead of relying on pure exploration that could be confusing or a long, annoying tutorial, the most sensible solution was a cover page with minimal, three-sentence context, which provided a glimpse into how the site worked while still allowing for exploration. Through this, I learned an important design lesson that authentic exploration requires guiding signal, not confusing ambiguity, and that music-based system needs precision, not random noise, to be recognized as intentional.

What I Learned

Balancing opposing values ​​requires extensive user testing. Because my initial thoughts are often different from the user's thoughts on the first experience:

  • In the prototype version, I thought that making the constellations hidden would be interesting and stimulating for users to explore, but in reality it was confusing and frustrating for users, because they had no idea how the site worked.
  • I thought that not having a tutorial would bring a nicer interface, have more space to interact, but it made difficulty for first-time users to experience the website, no solution to guide them if they got lost, leading to frustration and not wanting to interact anymore.

Ultimately, the balance comes from iterative testing and a willingness to cut unnecessariness. Some initial ideas had to be got rid of because they prioritized my perspective over the user experience. I also learned that playfulness works best when built on a foundation of clarity. Users need to understand how the site works before they can comfortably explore it. The “rules” must be clearly stated before the “game” can begin.

CONCLUSION

Sound Galaxy is my exploration project on spatial interaction systems and audio-visual synthesis. Through this project, I learned that random does not mean arbitrary, it even requires carefully considered limits, how to be random but not chaotic. In addition, user testing is very important in UI design, because real users can point out the shortcomings of the designer. And not every technique should be applied, because the most important thing is to create an experience, not just an interface. A beautiful interface but does not meet the criteria of usability and makes users uncomfortable, that product will fail.
