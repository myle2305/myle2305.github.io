  5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

    6. This interactive experience appears to be designed as a platform for open, visually driven exploration. The intended goal is to enable users to experience their own exploration of the visual aesthetics of the site through cursor movement.

  6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

    7. It communicates this through an interface with high contrast color palette and unique layout arrangement. The spontaneous rearrangement of images generated by movement and clicks reinforces the concept of discovery and unpredictability, rather than the consumption of structured content.

    8. The experience seems designed for short but repeated interactions. Users should be able to explore it briefly, leave, and return to try new interactions, rather than interacting with it in a single, continuous session.

  8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

    9. The lack of clear instructions coupled with the unpredictable interactions suggests an open-ended approach. Users are not guided through specific interaction steps but are instead encouraged to explore the color and layout movements and experience the aesthetic space of color within the website.
