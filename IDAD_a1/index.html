INTERACTION DESIGN ANALYSIS
on 7 Poems by Justine Nguyễn-Nguyễn & Tiger Dingsun
and Liquid Entrophy by Mika Chernov & Sinjin Hawke

Information Design

7 Poems

In "7 Poems", the artist created a clean, minimalist layout to avoid overloading information for users. Text gradually appears, line by line, as we click on the available green boxes, allowing us to focus our attention on each part of the story. By not using distracting elements like colorful images or animations, this website helps users have a deep experience and be focused on the story being told.

Liquid Entrophy

In contrast to the informational purpose of "7 Poems", "Liquid Entrophy" aims to create an environment for users to experience the tool. Therefore, the author has eliminated the usual information design, without specific instructional text. This helps users focus on the tool experience and encourages them to feel rather than receive information.

While "7 Poems" has a carefully organized layout that guides viewers to focus on receiving information, "Liquid Entropy" allows users to explore and experience the tool in their own way. This contrast is due to the difference in the purpose that the website author wants to aim for.

Mapping

7 Poems

Mapping in "7 Poems" is straightforward: clicking on the neatly arranged blue boxes on the screen to progress the story. The linear interaction ensures that the user always stays on track with the author’s intention. This keeps the user's experience of consuming information consistent and narrative.

Liquid Entrophy

"Liquid Entropy's" mapping is spatial and gestural. The website lets users create 3D abstract graphics and sounds by moving cursors. They also are allowed to adjust the feedback sound through the tool panel located on the right side of the screen. This arrangement prioritizes experience and exploration, and at the same time makes it easy to adjust the sound according to the user's expectations. This kind of mapping prioritizes immersion and creativity rather than practical use.

While "7 Poems" uses simple mapping to maintain narrative flow, "Liquid Entropy" uses open-ended mapping to create an interactive space.

Characterisation

7 Poems

In “7 Poems”, the interface’s characterisation is minimalist with the monochrome palette, centered text, and lack of decorative elements makes it formal. Interaction is quite slow and deliberate, each click reveals a small portion of text, creating a pace. In general, the interface is serious and intimate, which matches the emotionally heavy subject matter.

Liquid Entrophy

In “Liquid Entropy”, the interface’s characterisation is surreal, fluid, and immersive. It uses liquid-like textures, high-contrast colour gradients, and distorted 3D visuals that react instantly to user input. This creates creativity, mysterious, dreamy and full of discovery. Sound design is an important component that contributes to this personality, creating a deep impression on users about a vivid and different world.

“7 Poems” presents formal content by using minimal visuals and slow pacing to create an intimate atmosphere. “Liquid Entropy”, by contrast, embodies a surreal character, blending fluid visuals and feedback sound to evoke a mysterious, dreamlike world of exploration.

Feedback

7 Poems

“7 Poems” provides relatively gentle, rhythmic feedback that corresponds to each word as it appears. Users need to click on a word to create a pause. The rhythm of “7 Poems” feedback matches the overall mood of the poem, creating emotional tension. The feedback also fits the seriousness of the story, forcing the reader to follow the rhythm and engage with the poem. But it is also annoying if the sounds are mixed together if users forget to pause. Luckily, the site allows users to mute the sound in case they need a quiet space to ponder the content.

Liquid Entrophy

In “Liquid Entropy”, feedback is instantaneous and continuous. Cursor movement affects visuals and sounds in real time. This creates a responsive, almost musical interaction where the interface feels alive. Furthermore, users can adjust the feedback as desired by clicking or dragging sliders. It is unpredictable but engaging, rewarding curiosity. On the other hand, it is also annoying because there is no built-in mute button, which can be frustrating for the interactive explorer.

“7 Poems” offers gentle, rhythmic feedback that reflects the pace of the poem, fostering reflection but risking overlap if pauses are missed. “Liquid Entropy” delivers continuous, highly responsive audiovisual feedback that invites playful exploration, but the lack of a mute button can make the experience overwhelming.

Conclusion

“7 Poems” and “Liquid Entropy” reflect very different design intentions. “7 Poems” uses clarity, simplicity, and structure to support a powerful emotional narrative. “Liquid Entropy” uses ambiguity, immersion, and sensory feedback to create an open-ended, expressive experience. Each interface aligns its interaction design with its artistic goals, demonstrating how different principles can shape user experience in meaningful ways.
